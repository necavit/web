Some more things about me

(This was meant to be a blog post, but it did not fit really well there!)

I was born in Barcelona in 1992 (Olympics!!) and here I have lived ever since. It is a vibrant city, plenty of opportunities and experiences and awesome people from all over the world. I like it here, but I need to get outside from time to time, to let off steam and, mostly, because I love open air and nature in all its essence. The Pyrenees have been my second home for many years and there is nothing I like more than hiking up mountains, getting to the top and breathing deeply all the freedom you get there. And, speaking of mountains, I found a basque proverb in a mountaineering magazine that helps me keep going and pushing hard, either on a climb or, really, in any other situation:

Non gogoa, han zangoa.

Unfortunately, I don't actually speak basque, so forgive me if the translation is not accurate, but the idea is: "where there's a will, there's a way".

There is one other thing comparable to my love for mountains: cycling. I can't recall how old was I when I rode my first bike (my parents told me once that I was three), but I know it was an orange one! I've been mountain cycling through all these years, until I got myself a road bike almost a couple of years ago. And that was indeed a good decision! I had done a couple of short distance triathlons with my mountain bike, but the path for "long" distance ones finally opened with this one! On September 2014, I finished my first half ironman triathlon, together with some good friends of mine.

What do I do?

So, apart from enjoying a hard cycle climb or a relaxing mountain trek, how do I spend the rest of my day? Well, at the time of this writing, I am undertaking a bachelor degree in informatics engineering, with a major in computer science, and working at inLab FIB as an intern, developing Android and iOS apps. These are not the only things I've been involved in at the university campus - I was an active member of the school's students assembly for a couple of years and I was involved in the school's board too, as one of the (many) students representatives.
